Save them all!

resQmia.com is a true labor of love! I've been actively involved in animal rescue for years, and have always wanted to do more. When I heard my girlfriend, Mia (the director of operations of Alive Rescue) lament that she couldn't keep up with the 10 different spread sheets she uses to track the animals, the light bulb began to glow.

resQmia.com, built in Python and Django, allows for the tracking of animals through the rescue journey with reminders for vetting and necessary services. It supports image-upload and features are being added as quickly as I can respond to "I really need it to _______!!"

  • Date: January 2018
  • Deployment: www.resQmia.com, Digital Ocean, Linux Ubuntu
  • GitHub: https://github.com/baldmike/resQmia

Crosstown Classic

The 2005 World Champion Chicago White Sox host the 2016 World Champion Chicago Cubs in the game every Chicagoan has an opinion about... Written in Python/Django, relying heavily on session and taking a less-is-more approach to design, this game allows you to play 9 full innings, pitch by picth, responsively and interactively.

As a baseball lover and born-again tech nerd, this simple project is decades in the making for me and is the first thing I worked on upon completion of a coding bootcamp. I built and utilized a simple API to populate the teams with the 9 unique players that held that lineup position most often in that season. While each player currently has the same statistical outcomes, the ability for future customization of individual player's stats was written in.

Click the image above to play. Use the controls on the top left to pitch while on the field and either swing or watch the pitch while up to bat. Baserunners situationally advance automatically and all outcomes are statiscally ~VERY CLOSE!~ based on actual MLB statistics. Enjoy!

  • Date: December 2017
  • Tech: Python, Django, Bootstrap
  • Deployment: AWS and Digital Ocean, Linux Ubuntu
  • GitHub: https://github.com/baldmike/Crosstown-Classic

Wag Dog!

Lonely, dog?

Designed and presented for Coding Dojo's 'Project Week', WagDog began as a Match.com clone as a team project with cohort-mates Alyssa Nickow and Julian Auza. Once we learned that the dog dating community is grossly underserved, we put on our capes and set out to rectify that (and learned that there is a dating-site Lorem, providing hours of entertainment).

WagDog ("What a Good Dog!") allows a user to register, upload a picture and create a detailed personal profile. Once a profile is created, an algorithm matches the pup to dogs of similar lifestyles, hopes and dreams. If a butt-sniff is in order, the app allows you to send a "wink" or, for the slightly more brave breeds (usually Chihuahuas), in-app messaging to lay it on the line. Chihuahua coming on too strong? Block 'em!

Focusing on GitHub-based pair programming and project deadlines, 'Project Week' is intended to mimic the pressure and time constraints involved in delivering the MVP, as agreed on by the entire cohort. 'Finishing' WagDog a day early gave us the opportunity to slightly refactor some code and add a couple non-MVP features (messaging and user-block). Given more time, we all agreed that cleaning up the existing code would have taken preference over new, shiny features. But, hey, they work!

Click on the video for a short demo.

  • October 2017
  • Tech: C#, .NET core, Bootstrap
  • Deployment: Azure, Linux Ubuntu
  • GitHub: https://github.com/baldmike/WagDog
